What is the CTA Lime Line?

Post by Goat Moat, Sep 27, 2015.
Recent Transit

Transit isn't just about transportation, it's also amatter of marketing. When in troducing people to Chicago, you also have to think about what makes them drawn towards the city. The Loop! When you take transit out of the Loop not only thoroughfare, but also, marketing opportunities diminish as people become less attracted to the center.

We wouldn't be able to realistically imlement the Brown Line extension The reason we can't is because we have to dig into the already narrow street of Lawrence Ave and dig under for subways, on top of delaying traffic even more than it already is, which would cost millions of dollars and at minimum 2 years to complete. Plus, WHY extend the Brown Line when there's already a Lawrence Ave bus available?

The Lime Line extension is making use of transit that already exists. Turning Metra into CTA for an underserved part of Chicago, like the Red Line Extension, could be a great opportunity to being business and traffic to far off neighborhoods while at the same time making use of resources we have already.

A lot of these proposed connectors are old CTA train routes that are made pointless by CTA bus routes. 81 Lawrence, 49 Western & X49 Western Express, 9 Ashland & X9 Ashland 72 North, etc.
